# Judicial Document Anomaly Detection Skill

## Overview

Judicial Document Anomaly Detection Skill is an open-source AI Skill framework designed to assist large language models in analyzing potential anomalies within:

* Judicial judgments
* Arbitration awards
* Administrative decisions
* Case-handling records
* Hearing transcripts
* Evidence materials
* Legal correspondence

The project focuses on improving:

* Procedural transparency
* Reasoning consistency
* Evidence-response integrity
* Accessibility of legal analysis for ordinary citizens

Rather than replacing judges or legal professionals, this project aims to reduce the technical threshold required to identify possible issues inside legal documents and case-processing workflows.

---

## Core Idea

As AI-assisted judicial systems continue to expand, an important question emerges:

> How should society evaluate whether AI-assisted legal reasoning itself remains fair, objective, and procedurally consistent?

This Skill attempts to provide a practical answer by enabling general-purpose large language models to perform structured anomaly analysis on legal documents.

---

## What This Skill Detects

The Skill is designed to help identify possible:

* Fact-finding inconsistencies
* Missing responses to key evidence
* Procedural irregularities
* Logical discontinuities
* Selective evidence adoption
* Contradictory reasoning
* Inconsistent legal application
* Weak causality between findings and conclusions
* Administrative due-process anomalies
* Intra-case reasoning conflicts

## Design Philosophy

This project does NOT assume:

* every anomaly equals misconduct;
* every inconsistency implies intentional bias;
* AI can replace judicial discretion.

Instead, the project assumes:

* large-scale legal systems inevitably produce reasoning gaps;
* procedural fairness benefits from transparent analysis;
* AI can lower the cost of public supervision and legal understanding.

---

## Important Disclaimer

This project is an experimental AI-assisted analytical framework.

Outputs generated by LLMs are probabilistic interpretations, NOT legal conclusions.

The Skill should be used as:

* a supplementary review tool;
* a reasoning-audit assistant;
* a legal-document analysis aid.

It should NOT be treated as definitive legal advice or judicial determination.

---

## Vision

In the long run, this project hopes to contribute to:

* explainable AI-assisted adjudication;
* transparent legal reasoning;
* lower-cost procedural supervision;
* broader access to legal analytical capability.
